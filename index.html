Hi, I’m Harvey. A digital culture obsessive who probably spends a bit too much time on the internet...

Digital Project Manager, with a background in audio and entertainment.

Manchester, UK.

Here to work on projects with purpose – and to deliver them to audiences with creative, empathetic, and effective leadership.

I have over a decade of experience leading digital brands and campaigns in the UK audio and music industry – including roles at BBC Radio and Bauer Media – starting my career as a radio producer before specialising in social media. My background brings together content strategy, audience growth, and platform innovation – backed by a solid understanding of agile ways of working and leading cross-functional teams.

Most recently, I was the Senior Social Media Lead for BBC Radio 2, Radio 3 and 6 Music – overseeing creative output for the three national networks, shaping strategy with stakeholders, and empowering my extremely talented team to deliver over 1 billion organic video views in 2024.

Key projects included:

  • Coverage of flagship BBC live music events (Glastonbury Festival, Radio 2 in the Park, 6 Music Festival, BBC Proms)
  • Editorial and platform strategy refreshes for Radio 2 and 6 Music
  • Proposing and launching a modern new presence for Radio 3
  • The ‘Change The Tune’ moderation initiative on 6 Music – aimed at making online spaces more positive for musicians and fans.

Before that, I was BBC Radio Social's Senior Planner, leading portfolio-wide projects, evolving our approach to prioritisation and performance measurement, developing and testing tactics, and building valuable collaborations across the organisation.

After taking a career break to travel around Australia, I’m now excited to pursue a career pivot – applying my skills to a product-focused role in the tech space. Whether it’s project management, shaping and communicating digital strategy, driving growth, or team leadership – or a combination of all of those things, I'd love to chat about how I could fit into your team.
